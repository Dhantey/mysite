Volkswagen (German pronunciation: [ˈfɔlksˌvaːɡn̩] , abbreviated as VW, is a German motor vehicle manufacturer headquartered
in Wolfsburg, Lower Saxony, Germany. Founded in 1937 by the German Labour Front
the Nazi Party, it is known for the iconic Beetle and serves
as the flagship brand of the Volkswagen Group , the largest automotive
manufacturer by worldwide sales in 2016 and 2017. The group's biggest market is in China, which delivers
40 percent of its sales and profits. Its name is derived from the German-language
terms Volk and Wagen, translating to "people's car" when combined.
